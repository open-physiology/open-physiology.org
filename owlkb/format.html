RICORDO data formats

Two Types of Data

There are two types of data:

  1. Knowledgebase (KB)
    • Ontology
    • Determines the treemap structure
    • Format: OWL
    • Size: relatively small (10s of thousands of terms usually)
    • Supported by RICORDO's "OWLKB" product
  2. Annotations
    • Triples
    • Adds individual facts
    • Format: RDF (N-Triples or...)
    • Size: possibly large (in principle can include billions of facts)
    • Supported by RICORDO's RDFStore product (note that RDFStore is currently just a thin proxy in front of a so-called triplestore such as Virtuoso, which does the real heavy lifting, so if you don't need RDFStore's features, it might be better to work directly with Virtuoso).

It is helpful to think of a GoogleMaps analogy.

  1. Knowledgebase defines which cities are in which states, etc...
  2. Annotations indicate locations of individual restaurants, libraries, etc...

Knowledgebase data format

Knowledgebase data uses the OWL/XML format. There are two possibilities.
  1. Use an existing, open-source ontology.
    • If so, no need to worry about this specific data format.
    • For example, the apiNATOMY demo instance uses FMA, an ontology of anatomy terms.
  2. Roll your own ontology.
    • May be lots of work.
    • Offers maximum control over the treemap structure.

If you choose to roll your own ontology, click here for an outline of a bare-bones OWL file (opens in new tab).

If you choose to use existing ontology/ontologies, you should "wrap" them up in your own CREST ontology. This is simple; click here for a basic wrapper ontology that you can edit as needed (opens in new tab).

Annotations data format

  • Annotations consist of "triples".
  • A "triple" has three parts:
    1. subject
    2. predicate
    3. object
  • Example: An annotation saying "Item 555 is an image of a brain" might be like so:
    1. Subject: <http://crest.org/items#CREST_555>
    2. Predicate: <http://crest.org/items#image_of>
    3. Object: <http://some-ontology-homepage.org/version_info/FMA_50801>
    (Assuming your knowledgebase includes an ontology with a term FMA_50801 for "brain")

There are various different formats for triples.

    » We recommend the N-Triples format, as it is by far the simplest option.
